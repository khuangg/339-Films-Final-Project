My Favorite Romantic Comedies

10 Things I Hate About You (1999)

My Review: 5/5

My Thoughts: Although many may think that this film is "predictable", that is just what makes it better. The film starts off strong with a classic banger, "Bad Reputation" by Joan Jett & the Blackhearts and that alone set the pace of the film. It is a classic romantic comedy featuring a golden retriver boy who everyone wishes was theirs including a bad boy that viewers falls in love with. Yet, I still enjoy it everytime I watch it.

Director: Gil Junger

Rated: PG-13

Summary: On the first day at his new school, Cameron instantly falls for Bianca, the gorgeous girl of his dreams. The only problem is the Bianca is forbidden to date until her ill-tempered, completely un-dateable older sister Kat goes out, too. In an attempt to solve his problem, Cameron singles out the only guy who could possibly be a match for Kat: a mysterious bad boy with a nasty reputation of his own.

Crazy Rich Asians (2018)

My Review: 5/5

My Thoughts: This film is SO important to me. Everything about it was so magical. The acting, wardrobe, location, just everything was perfect. It was amazing to see the asian culture protrayed and appreciated on the big screen.

Director: Jon M. Chu

Rated: PG-13

Summary: An American-born Chinese economics professor accompanies her boyfriend to Singapore for this best friend's wedding, only to get thrust into the lives of Asia's rich and famous.

Life as We Know It (2010)

My Review: 5/5

My Thoughts: I've probably watched this movie around 50 times because of how good it is. The typical enemies to friends to lovers. Everything about this movie brings me happiness, especially Josh Duhamel.

Director: Greg Berlanti

Rated: PG-13

Summary: After a distastrous first date for caterer Holly and network sports director Messer, all they have in common is a dislike for each other and their love for their goddaughter Sophie. But when they suddenly become all Sophie has in this world, Holly and Messer must set their differences aside. Juggling careers and social calendars, they'll have to find common ground while living under the same roof.

Love, Rosie (2014)

My Review: 5/5

My Thoughts: Love, Rosie touches on the relationship between two best friends and how they navigate through life. The best friends to lovers is the perfect combination.

Director: Christian Ditter

Rated: R

Summary: Since the moment they met at age 5, Rosie and Alex have been best friends, facing the highs and lows of growing up side by side. A fleeting shared moment, one missed opportunity, and the decisions that follow send their lives in completely different directions. As each navigates the complexities of life, love, and everything in between, they always find their way back to each other - but is it just friendship or something more? Based on Cecelia Ahern's bestselling novel, Love, Rosie is a heart warming, modern comedy-of-errors posing the ultimate questions: do we really only get one shot at true love?

The Proposal (2009)

My Review: 4/5

My Thoughts: Whenever Ryan Reynolds pops up on my screen, I am instantly intrigued. Add Sandra Bullock into that, a must-watch.

Director: Anne Fletcher

Rated: PG-13

Summary: When she learns she's in danger of losing her visa status and being deported, overbearing book editor Margaret Tate forces her put-upon assistant, Andrew Paxton, to marry her.

New Year's Eve (2011)

My Review: 3.5/5

My Thoughts: I love seeing the ending of these films because you realize how the characters intertwine with each other. I love a good ensemble cast.

Director: Garry Marshall

Rated: PG-13

Summary: The lives of several couples and singles in New York intertwine over the course of New Year's Eve.

Friends with Benefits (2011)

My Review: 4/5

My Thoughts: Yet another predictable, entertaining film that I am not ashamed of loving. I think this is one of my guilty pleasure movies because even though it is predictable, it still got me crying and wanting more.

Director: Will Gluck

Rated: R

Summary: Dylan and Jamie think it's going to be easy to add the simple act of sex to their friendship, despite what Hollywood romantic comedies would have them believe. They soon discover however that getting physical really does always lead to complications.

How to Lose a Guy in 10 Days (2003)

My Review: 5/5

This movie got me crying for HOURS. I love a good Matthew McConaughey romantic comedy film. My favorite plot is when two enemies end up falling in love with each other and this film plays that out PERFECTLY.

Director: Donald Petrie

Rated: PG-13

Summary: An advice columnist, Andie Anderson, tries pushing the boundaries of what she can write about in her new piece about how to get a man to leave you in 10 days. Her editor, Lana, loves it, and Andie goes off to find a man she can use for the experiment. Enter executive Ben Berry, who is so confident in his romantic prowess that the thinks he can make any woman fall in love with him in 10 days. When Andie and Ben meet, their plans backfire.

27 Dresses (2008)

My Review: 5/5

My Thoughts: 27 Dresses was an amazing movie. I thought the plot was very unique like she made 27 dresses? That is crazy. And that bar scene?? Perfection and so iconic.

Director: Anne Fletcher

Rated: PG-13

Summary: Altruistic Jane finds herself facing her worst nightmare as her younger sister announces her engagement to the man Jane secretly adores.

Clueless (1995)

My Review: 4.5/5

My Thoughts: Classic. Iconic. Original. Paul Rudd. All things perfect.

Director: Amy Heckerling

Rated: PG-13

Summary: Shallow, rich and socially successful Cher is at the top of her Beverly Hill high school's pecking scale. Seeing herself as a matchmaker, Cher first coaxes two teachers into dating each other. Emboldened by her success, she decides to give hopeless klutzy new student Tai a makeover. When Tai becomes more popular than she is, Cher realizes that her disapproving ex-stepbrother was right about how misguided she was -- and falls for him.
